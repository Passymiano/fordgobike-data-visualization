What is the structure of your dataset?¶

There are 183, 412 individual bike trips in the dataset with 16 features which inciude: duration_sec, start_time, end_time, end_time, start_station_id, start_station_nam, start_station_latitude start_station_longitude end_station_id end_station_name end_station_latitude end_station_longitude, bike_id, user_type, member_birth_year, member_gender, bike_share_for_all_trip

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I am most interested in features that can give me insights on the demands of bike rides and discovering trends in bike usage in the dataset.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

I expect types of users, start_time and duration to give me insights on bike usage trends.

Conclusions¶

The analysis was very insightful and some extra effort had to be put in to ensure the variables of interest had the correct datatype and I also had to extract some variables from the start_time variable to make my analysis comprehensive. Some feature engineering had to performed also to get the age of the users. Subsequently, this are my main insights:

  1. Customers make longer bike rides.
  2. There is a spike in bike rides during commute hours.
  3. There are fewer bike rides during the weekends however they are longer than other days of the week
  4. There are more male riders than female riders. However, female riders take longer bike rides than male riders.
